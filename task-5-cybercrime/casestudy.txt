# Case Study: UPI Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common cybercrime in India where scammers trick users into transferring money through mobile payment apps. This type of fraud usually happens through phone calls, fake links, or QR codes.

In a typical scenario, a scammer contacts the victim pretending to be a bank official, delivery agent, or customer support executive. They create urgency by saying there is a problem with the user’s account or a pending payment. The victim is then asked to click on a malicious link or scan a QR code. In some cases, the scammer sends a “collect request” and instructs the user to enter their UPI PIN, claiming it is needed to receive money. However, entering the PIN actually authorizes a payment from the victim’s account.

Students, elderly people, and individuals who are not very familiar with digital payments are common targets. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and loss of trust in online services.

Such frauds are increasing due to the growing use of digital payments. Awareness and caution are essential to prevent falling victim to these scams.
